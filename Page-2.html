BMSCE, BANGLORE 

                         Bhusanayana Mukundadas Sreenivasaiah Institution of Engineering (BMSCE), also known as B.M.S. College of Engineering, is a private engineering college in Basavanagudi, Bangalore. The college was founded by Bhusanayana Mukundadas Sreenivasaiah in 1946 and the B.M.S Educational Trust is in charge of its administration. BMS College of Engineering offers courses in the UG, PG, and PhD levels. B.E. is the flagship program of BMSCE. The institute also offers PhD and MSc (Engineering by Research) programs. 14 departments have been designated as Research Centers. Admissions to all the courses offered at BMSCE are on the basis of various state and national-level entrance tests. These include PGCET, KMAT, MAT, GATE, etc.There are additional counseling sessions for seat distribution in some courses. Students must register for a course twice to get admission at BMSCE, once at the entrance exam’s official website and next at the official website of BMSCE. (BMSCE), also known as B.M.S. Admission

The admission process for BMS College of Engineering begins in the month of January with the commencement of registrations for MBA and MCA courses. The application process for UG courses begins in the month of February. The application process is offline and the candidates have to take a printout of the application form and submit it before the final date. The application forms are available in the college’s website. The admissions to UG courses are granted on the basis of COMEDK, the admissions to MBA and MCA are granted on the basis of KMAT, and the admissions to M.Tech. and M.Arch courses are granted on the basis of PGCET. Any relaxation in academic eligibility is extended only to Karnataka candidates belonging to the Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe and Other Backward Classes. he admissions to B.Tech and B.E. courses are granted on the basis of candidate’s merit in Consortium of Medical, Engineering and Dental Colleges of Karnataka Undergraduate Entrance Test (COMEDK UGET). The entrance test will have 180 multiple choice questions of 1 mark each. The exam will have three sections: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Each section will have 60 questions each. The candidates will get 3 hours to complete the exam. There will be no negative marking. However, in the event of a tie in the test score the principle of least negative responses (Number of wrong answers) will be adopted. In addition, if required, other methods may be adopted to break ties. The entrance test is based on the existing syllabus of 10 + 2 / 12th STD / 2nd PUC. There shall be no Entrance Test for candidates seeking admission to B.Arch Course. Candidates seeking admission to B. Arch course shall submit separate application form for counseling as per the notification on COMEDK website (will be made available before counseling).
